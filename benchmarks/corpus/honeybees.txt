Honey bees are social insects that live in colonies with a single queen. A healthy honey bee colony can contain between 20000 and 80000 worker bees in summer. Worker bees communicate the location of food sources through a behavior called the waggle dance. Honey bees pollinate many crops, including apples, almonds, and blueberries. A queen honey bee can lay more than 1500 eggs in a single day during peak season.
